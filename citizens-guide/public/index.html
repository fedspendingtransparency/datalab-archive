In 2017,
the federal governmentcollected $3.3 trillion.

The federal government collects revenue from a variety of sources, including individual income taxes, payroll taxes, corporate income taxes, and excise taxes. It also collects revenue from services like admission to national parks and customs duties.

What is $3.3 trillion equivalent to?

The federal government brings in $105,000 of revenue per second.

In the time it takes you to read this statement, the federal government has collected roughly $1 million.

$3.3 trillion equates to 68 million individuals earning roughly $49,000 per year.

That is equal to the average salary of someone in the construction industry.

Source - Bureau of Labor Statistics

$3.3 trillion is equal to a little more than $10,000 per individual in the United States (estimated at 326.4 million).

Source - U.S. Census Bureau

$3.3 trillion equates to the average household contributing $2,200 per month.

That is roughly equal to renting a three-bedroom apartment outside Tampa, FL ($2,199 per month).

Source - Department of Housing and Urban Development & U.S. Census Bureau

$3.3 trillion equates to the revenue of the top 18 companies in the S&P 500.

Figures based on 2017 10-K reports for S&P 500 Companies

If the revenue of the federal government was split between the 126 million households estimated in the United States in 2017. It would equate to $26,000 per household.

Source - U.S. Census Bureau

In 2017,
the federal government
spent $670 billion more than it collected,resulting in a deficit.

The difference between what the federal government collects and what it spends is referred to as a deficit or surplus. In 2017, the federal government spent more than it collected, resulting in a deficit.

What is $660 billion equivalent to?

$670 billion is almost 11 times the amount of credit card debt Americans created in 2017 ($61 billion).

$670 billion breaks down to about $5,000 of deficit spending per household in 2017.

In 2017, there were an estimated amount of 126 million households in the United States.

$670 billion equates to $1 million in deficit spending every minute for an entire year.

In 2017,
the public debtincreased to $20.2 trillion.

To pay for a deficit, the federal government usually takes on debt. The public debt is essentially the amount that the government currently owes from deficits and other liabilities that have accumulated over time.

Are debt and deficit the same thing? No, but they do affect one another.
