In 2017,
the deficit was $660 billion.

The difference between what the federal government collects and what it spends is referred to as a deficit or surplus. In 2017, the federal government spent more than it collected, resulting in a deficit.

What is $660 billion equivalent to?

It's 10 times the amount of credit card debt Americans created in 2017 ($61 billion).

In 2017, the U.S. Census Bureau estimated that there were 126,224,000 households in the United States. That breaks down to about $2,000 of deficit spending per household.

It equates to adding $1 million in deficit spending every minute.

In 2017,
the national debt was $20.2 trillion.

To pay for a deficit, the federal government usually takes on debt. The total debt that the federal government owes is essentially the accumulation of deficits over time.

Are debt and deficit the same thing? No, but they do affect one another.
