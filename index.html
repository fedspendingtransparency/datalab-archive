Explore Federal Programs That Address Homelessness

On a single night in 2016, over 500,000 people were experiencing homelessness in the United States. One out of every five of these people were children. Use this visualization to explore the federal government’s approach to addressing homelessness in America.

Who Receives Federal Contracts?

Did you know that the federal government spends about $500 billion each year on contracts? This is around the size of the economy of Sweden! Explore contract recipients and what agencies are getting with these contracts, from missiles to lighting fixtures to internet and phone service.

Discover the Federal Government’s Bank Accounts

Did you know that the government has almost 2,000 bank accounts? These accounts vary greatly in size across the government, ranging from a few thousand dollars to tens of billions. Use this interactive tree-style visualization to explore these accounts and see how much money has been spent recently.

See How The Government Categorizes Its Purchases

The DATA Act now allows you to connect two different categorization systems: what the government pays for and what it is trying to achieve. For example, you can use this visualization to see when money is spent for national defense, the amounts spent on military personnel versus military contractors.

What Do Federal Employees Do And Where Do They Live?
